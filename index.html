The quest for ethical coding

My journey into coding is as much driven by my frustration with the state of the tech industry as it is by my interest in it. Over the years, the more I logged into social media, the angrier I became. I felt as though I was constantly being manipulated by the things I read into feeling angry all the time. Knowing what I do now about how Facebook write their algorithms to keep us coming back for more (and keep us divided), I now understand that the way social media managed to keep me engaged was to keep me angry and in debate 24/7. Recent documentaries such as 'The Social Dilemma' further drove this message home for me.

With this insight into the world of unethical coding, a desire to be part of a movement that contributes to making a positive impact in tech, rather than one that manipulates and divides people, has manifested. I say, without the slightest hint of hyperbole, that social media robbed me of my empathy. By disarming my anger and embracing reason through studying Moral Philosophy (Applied Ethics), where I asked difficult questions as to how to best deal with the social and moral issues I cared about, I was able to gain my empathy back and use it in a constructive, rational manner.
